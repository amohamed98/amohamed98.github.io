Hi! My name is Abdallah.

I am a Ph.D. Student and NSF-GRFP Fellow at MIT Biology.

My research interets surround how cellular and developmental identities are established; specifically how chromatin organization and RNA processing come together in order to help establish proper cellular identity.

Previously I recieved my B.A. ('20) in Biology from Columbia University. At Columbia I was an undergraduate student in the Lomvardas Lab where I studied the biochemical mechanisms by which long-range interactions between heterchromatically silenced gene loci are established and maintained throughout olfactory sensory neuron development.

Additionally I was previsouly an Amgen Scholar in the Narlikar Lab where I studied the liquid-droplet properties of heterochromatin associated proteins.
