Hi! My name is Abdallah.

I am a Ph.D. Student and NSF-GRFP Fellow at MIT Biology.

My research interets focus on how cellular and developmental identities are established; specifically the role chromatin organization and RNA processing play in establishing cell identity.

Before MIT, I recieved my B.A. ('20) in Biology from Columbia University. At Columbia I was a member of the Lomvardas Lab where I studied chromatin organization in olfactory sensory neurons. Additionally I was an Amgen Scholar in the Narlikar Lab at UCSF where I studied the liquid-droplet properties of heterochromatin associated proteins.
